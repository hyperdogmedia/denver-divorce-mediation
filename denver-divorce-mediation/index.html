How Long Does an Uncontested Divorce Take?

Denver Divorce Mediation

If you and your spouse have negotiated an uncontested divorce, you might be eager to have it processed and completed as soon as you can. In the end, very few things in life are so painful and arduous as a divorce that drags on forever. You've probably been told that Denver divorce mediation are able to help you move forward quicker than going through a court hearing, as long as both you and your soon-to be ex-partner make an effort to stay in the same direction. How long should you expect it to take from filing to completing?

In reality, there's no specific answer to that question. It's difficult to establish a timeline for an uncontested divorce as each case is different. It could be that you experience a more lengthy or less ad hoc mediation than a couple. There are many factors that determine the duration of divorce.

Co-operation

The divorce mediation process will take much less time if are able to cooperate with your soon to be ex-spouse instead of than fighting on every point. You're more likely to end your divorce uncontested quicker if you're able to reach compromises and work together to find mutually beneficial solutions to every issue.

Maintain control of your emotions

Staying calm during divorce mediation can also speed up the process. While it's natural for emotions to rise in this tumultuous time It is important to put aside any feelings of anger, sadness, or grief triggered due to divorce to concentrate on the job in hand. This method is beneficial for both of the parties in the end.

Unique Circumstances

Couples with many assets like property, stocks, or other important possessions could take longer to complete uncontested divorce mediation in comparison to couples with fewer assets. Moreover, if a couple has pets or children and are unable to reach an satisfactory custody agreement, the process may also be delayed. Collaborating to create a custody schedule that benefits both parents and children can speed up the process of mediation.

After you've agreed on the terms of your divorce, the mediator will assist you in filing the paperwork with your county or state. From this point there's a 91 day mandatory waiting period prior to when the state of Colorado will allow divorce. It's in the best interests of each of the parties to work in order for a swift mediation process.

Uncontested divorce might be the best option for you and your soon-to-be ex-spouse, if you'd like to reduce time along with money and emotional stress. Divorce mediation is a better alternative to traditional legal proceedings in court. It will allow you to accelerate your divorce and start the next chapter in your life faster.
